Internet of Things, Digital Biomarker, and Artificial Intelligence in Spine: Current and Future Perspectives

https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6944984/ December 31,2019

AI is gaining significant attention in almost every field and this article talks about the relation of Medical Industry with AI. Medical AI can be useful for many purposes such as diagnosing diseases, predicting outcomes of operations etc. A device called Digital Biomarker is introduced, which is a wearable device that will be used to monitor physical and mental health, spinal posture etc.

Unravelling the Secrets of Plants: Emerging Wearable Sensors for Plants Signals and Physiology

https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/adsr.202300023 29 May, 2023

The article focuses on monitoring plant physiology using wearable sensors, in order to increase the agricultural production. This new technique will analyze stomatal functions and volatile movements of plants, and it would be a cheaper way than traditional techniques. Moreover, it will not interfere with natural movements and processes of plants.

Genetically engineered microbes make their own fertilizer, could feed the world's poorest

https://www.science.org/content/article/genetically-engineered-microbes-make-their-own-fertilizer-could-feed-world-s-poorest 4 April, 2017

The article is all about increasing production of plants with the help of new type of industrial fertilizers that researchers have engineered. They are microbes that, when added to soil, make fertilizer on demand, producing plants that grow 1.5 times larger than crops.
